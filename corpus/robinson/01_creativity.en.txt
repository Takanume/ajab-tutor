# theme: Schools kill creativity — and what that means for your future

The school system you're living through right now was designed in the 19th century to produce factory workers and clerks. Not to produce humans capable of thinking. When subjects are ranked — math and sciences at the top, languages just below, and then at the bottom music, art, drama — there's no scientific basis for that hierarchy. It's just an inheritance from the era when we needed a certain type of worker.

If you feel "average" or "bad" at school, ask yourself first: average or bad by WHICH criteria? The system chose one very specific type of intelligence — abstract, sequential, written — and tells you that THIS is intelligence. It's wrong. Intelligence is broader: there's musical intelligence, kinesthetic, interpersonal, spatial. You're not "bad at school." You might be brilliant in a direction the school doesn't measure.

This doesn't mean drop out. It means understand that what you're learning right now is a transit point, not a measure of your worth. The real question isn't "am I good at math?" It's "what can I do with what I'm learning that would actually make me feel alive?"

You're not learning for the exam. You're learning to have options. The exam is a tollbooth on the road, not the destination.
